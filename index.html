The consent decrees that govern New York City

Decades of litigation have left New York City under standing court orders covering its jails, police, housing, schools, shelters, and child welfare system. This database collects what's still active, what each decree requires, and how it shapes day-to-day operations.

About this database

A consent decree is a court-approved settlement that becomes a binding court order. New York City has accumulated dozens of them since the 1970s, each one re-shaping how an agency must operate. This site organizes the major ones still in force, with primary sources, monitor reports, and plain-language explanations.
